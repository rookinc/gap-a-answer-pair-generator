\begin{abstract}
This paper formulates the Gap A answer-pair generator problem arising from the WXYZTI reciprocal overlay audit in Project 18. The seed result is exact but bounded: within a realized row-pair candidate universe of 48 pairings, the reciprocal answer-pair grammar selects exactly the 12 realized answer-pairs, with zero false positives and zero misses. This paper asks whether that result can be lifted to a native generator: a rule that produces the relevant candidate universe from source structure without using the realized pair labels as input. At the outset, Gap A remains open.
\end{abstract}
