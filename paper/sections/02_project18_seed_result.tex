\section{Project 18 seed result}

The seed result is the WXYZTI reciprocal pair grammar found after the Project 18 kernel-admission theorem.

The realized overlay contains 24 station rows. These split into two role classes:
\[
\texttt{reverse\_partner}
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
\texttt{shared\_B}.
\]

The reverse-partner rows satisfy the exact transform
\[
(A,B,C)\mapsto(A,C,B).
\]
The shared-\(B\) rows preserve \(B\) and the slot coordinate.

The key result is paired rather than single-row. Each realized directed transition is represented by a reciprocal pair \((S,R)\), where \(S\) is a shared-\(B\) row and \(R\) is a reverse-partner row. The cross-coupling law is
\[
S.C_{\mathrm{to}}=R.B_{\mathrm{from}}=R.\mathrm{slot}_{\mathrm{from}},
\]
and
\[
R.B_{\mathrm{to}}=S.C_{\mathrm{from}},
\qquad
R.\mathrm{slot}_{\mathrm{to}}=S.C_{\mathrm{from}}.
\]

A row-pair candidate universe was then formed by crossing every realized shared-\(B\) row with every realized reverse-partner row in the same role-pair channel. This produced 48 candidate pairings. The reciprocal answer-pair selector accepted exactly the 12 realized pairs:
\[
\text{accepted}=12,\qquad
\text{false positives}=0,\qquad
\text{misses}=0.
\]

This is the seed result for the present paper.
