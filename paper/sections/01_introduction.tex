\section{Introduction}

Project 18 closed a bounded kernel-admission theorem for a 900-vertex graph candidate. After that graph-level admission package closed, a local WXYZTI overlay audit exposed a sharper witness structure. The overlay rows did not behave as isolated transitions. They organized into reciprocal pairs.

This paper begins from that observation. Its purpose is not to reprove the graph-admission theorem. Its purpose is to formulate and test the next problem:

\[
\text{Can the reciprocal WXYZTI answer-pair universe be generated natively?}
\]

The working claim is intentionally cautious. The pair grammar is exact over the realized row-pair candidate universe. It is not yet a Gap A closure theorem, because the candidate universe used so far is still built from realized WXYZTI rows.

The project target is therefore a generator theorem. If such a generator is found, the paper may be renamed as a closure paper. Until then, the paper records a disciplined search for the native rule.
